Multiscale Chemical Reaction Networks and Piecewise deterministic Markov Processes

This animations demonstrate how a multiscale population converges to a piecewise deterministic Markov process (PDMP). In particular consider the reaction network given by \[\begin{align} X_1+Y_1&\underset{\alpha}{\to} X_2+Y_1\\ X_2 +Y_2&\underset{\beta}{\to} X_1+Y_2\\ Y_1&\underset{\lambda}{\to} Y_2\\ Y_2&\underset{\mu}{\to} Y_1. \end{align}\] In this reaction scheme the $ Y_i$ species catalyses the conversion of the $X_i$ species. If the conserved quantity $X_1+X_2 = r$ becomes large while $Y_1+Y_2= l$ remains fixed the dynamics governing the concentrations $[X_i]$ becomes deterministic in between fluctuations in $Y_i$. More specifically, it has been shown that in the limit $r \to \infty$, $X_ir^{-1} \to Z_i$ where $Z_i$ is a PDMP, where here the convergence is in distribution.
